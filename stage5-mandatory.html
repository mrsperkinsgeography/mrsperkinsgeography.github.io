NSW Geography 7-10 Syllabus (2024)

Stage 5 Mandatory Course

Four focus areas. Years 9 and 10. Teaching resources for each unit, built for non-specialist teachers.

These pages support the Geography 7-10 Syllabus (2024), implemented from 2027. Each focus area page includes a lesson sequence, key ideas, case study suggestions, assessment ideas and vocabulary - written so any teacher can pick it up and use it. Read the full NESA syllabus ↗
Focus area 1
Changing Places
Why do places look completely different from one generation to the next - and who decides what they become?
What students study
How and why places change over time through population movement, urban growth, economic shifts and government decisions. Who benefits from change, and who is left behind.

Focus area 2
Biomes and Sustainable Agriculture
How do we feed 8 billion people - and what happens when the biomes we depend on can no longer keep up?
What students study
The world's major biomes, the agricultural systems that sit within them, and the pressures of climate change, population growth and global food demand on sustainable production.
Key geographical concepts
EnvironmentSustainabilityInterconnectionScaleChange
Links to the SDGs
SDG 2 - Zero HungerSDG 15 - Life on Land

Focus area 3
Environmental Change and Management
When an environment changes faster than anything living in it can adapt, what can actually be done?
What students study
How environments change through natural and human-induced processes, the consequences for ecosystems and communities, and the range of management responses available at local, national and global scales.

Focus area 4
Human Wellbeing
Why is where you are born still the single biggest factor in determining how long and how well you will live?
What students study
How human wellbeing is measured, why it varies so dramatically between and within countries, and what governments, organisations and individuals are doing to close the gap.
