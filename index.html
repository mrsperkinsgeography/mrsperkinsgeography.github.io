Step 1

Choose your film

Read each description. Pick the one that speaks to you. All three are available on ClickView — no login needed to view this page.

Film 1

2040

Dir. Damon Gameau · Australia, 2019 · G · 92 min

Australian filmmaker Damon Gameau had a newborn daughter and was terrified about the world she would inherit. Rather than make a film about how bad things are, he travelled the world to find the solutions that already exist.

Hopeful. Solutions-focused. Personal. Australian.

Best SDG links: 7 · 11 · 13 · 15

Ask yourself: Do I want to feel hopeful about what's possible?

Film 2

Before the Flood

Leonardo DiCaprio · Nat Geo, 2016 · PG · 96 min

As a UN Messenger of Peace, DiCaprio travels to the world's most climate-affected places — and asks one uncomfortable question: why, despite knowing about climate change for decades, do we keep failing to act?

Urgent. Political. Globally-minded.

Best SDG links: 7 · 13 · 15 · 17

Ask yourself: Do I want to understand why change is so hard?

Film 3

Drowning in Plastic

Liz Bonnin · BBC One, 2018 · All ages · 60 min

Wildlife biologist Liz Bonnin investigates ocean plastic — starting on Lord Howe Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site just 400km off the NSW coast, where seabirds are dying from plastic they've eaten.

Emotional. Visually stunning. Australian connection.

Best SDG links: 6 · 12 · 14 · 9

Ask yourself: Do I want to understand what's in our own backyard?

The 17 Goals

UN Sustainable Development Goals

In 2015, every country in the world agreed on these 17 goals for a better, fairer, more sustainable world by 2030. You'll choose one that connects to your film in Lesson 3.

1 No Poverty 2 Zero Hunger 3 Good Health 4 Quality Education 5 Gender Equality 6 Clean Water 7 Clean Energy 8 Decent Work 9 Innovation 10 Reduced Inequality 11 Sustainable Cities 12 Responsible Consumption 13 Climate Action 14 Life Below Water 15 Life on Land 16 Peace & Justice 17 Partnerships
