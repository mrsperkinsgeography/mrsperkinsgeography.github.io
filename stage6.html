NSW Geography · 2022 Syllabus

Stage 6 Geography

Years 11 and 12 - what you'll study, how you'll be assessed, and what the HSC looks like.

"Year 11 builds the foundation. Year 12 is where you use it."

Year 11 is 120 hours across four focus areas. You study all four, and you complete at least 12 hours of fieldwork across the year.

What you'll study
Focus area 1
Earth's Natural Systems
40 hours
How the Earth works as an integrated physical system - landscapes, cycles and the natural processes that shape land and water.
Focus area 2
People, Patterns and Processes
40 hours
How humans have transformed the Earth's surface, and what makes one place different from another.
Your class will focus on one chosen study within this topic.
Focus area 3
Human-Environment Interactions
20 hours
How natural systems and human activity interact - land cover change, climate change and the long-term consequences of how we have used the Earth.
Your class will focus on one chosen study: a geographic region, a contemporary hazard or climate change.
Focus area 4
Geographical Investigation
20 hours
Your own geographical inquiry - you choose the question, collect data in the field, and present your findings.
What is the Geographical Investigation?
Your own research - in Year 11
The Geographical Investigation is a school-based research task in Year 11. It does not appear in the HSC exam. You may have heard it called a "Depth Study" - that was the old syllabus name.
You choose a geographical question, plan your investigation, collect real data and communicate your findings. It takes around 20 hours and is assessed by your school as part of your Year 11 grade.
Fieldwork: 12 hours are mandatory in Year 11, spread across the course.
How you'll be assessed in Year 11

Year 11 is entirely school-based - there is no external exam at the end of Year 11. Your school sets at least three formal tasks across the year. Your teacher uses all of that evidence to determine an overall grade.

Component
40%
Knowledge and understanding
Your understanding of geographical content across all four focus areas
Component
20%
Geographical skills and tools
Maps, graphs, data, fieldwork instruments and spatial technologies
Component
20%
Inquiry and research
Including the Geographical Investigation and fieldwork
Component
20%
Communication
How clearly and accurately you communicate geographical ideas in appropriate forms

"Year 12 is where Year 11 pays off. The HSC rewards students who think clearly and write precisely."

Year 12 is 120 hours across three focus areas - you study all three. Year 11 content is assumed knowledge. The HSC exam is three hours and covers all of Year 12.

What you'll study
Focus area 1
Global Sustainability
30 hours
What sustainability means in practice, and whether a real global economic activity is actually achieving it.
Your class will study one global economic activity and evaluate its sustainability.
Focus area 2
Rural and Urban Places
45 hours
How settlements work and change - from small rural towns to megacities - and the forces reshaping places globally.
Your class will study one rural place, one urban place and one large city outside Australia.
Focus area 3
Ecosystems and Global Biodiversity
45 hours
How ecosystems function, what they are worth and what is happening to global biodiversity.
Your class will study two ecosystem types, with at least one from outside Australia.
Fieldwork: 12 hours are mandatory in Year 12. One school-based task must be based on a fieldwork activity.
How you'll be assessed in Year 12

Year 12 has two parts: school-based assessment tasks set by your school throughout the year, and the external HSC exam in October. Your school submits a mark to NESA based on your tasks; this is combined with your exam mark to produce your final HSC mark. The weighting of that combination is set by NESA across all HSC courses - check the NESA website for the current details.

School component
40%
Knowledge and understanding
Across all three Year 12 focus areas
School component
20%
Skills and tools
Maps, graphs, GIS, fieldwork instruments and data
School component
20%
Inquiry and research
Including the mandatory fieldwork-based task
School component
20%
Communication
Communicating geographical understanding in appropriate forms
The HSC exam
Geography HSC Examination - 100 marks, 3 hours
Section I - 15 marks
Multiple choice
15 multiple choice questions using stimulus material. About 30 minutes.
Section II - 45 marks
Short answer
4 to 6 short-answer questions, some with multiple parts. About 1 hour 20 minutes.
Section III - 20 marks
Structured extended response
A structured extended response on either Rural and Urban Places or Ecosystems and Global Biodiversity. About 35 minutes.
Section IV - 20 marks
Extended response
An extended response on whichever of the two topics was not examined in Section III. About 35 minutes.
Global Sustainability is examined in Sections I and II. Sections III and IV cover Rural and Urban Places and Ecosystems and Global Biodiversity - one each, but you will not know which is which until you open the paper.
